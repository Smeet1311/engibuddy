# Barak 5-Phase STEM Education Framework

The Barak framework provides a simplified but rigorous 5-phase structure for STEM Project-Based Learning, emphasizing investigative thinking and systematic problem-solving.

## Phase 1: IDENTIFY
**Purpose:** Recognize and articulate the engineering/science problem or challenge.

**Characteristics:**
- Define the problem clearly and specifically
- Establish success criteria and constraints
- Identify stakeholders and beneficiaries
- Understand the context and background

**Key Questions:**
- What is the actual problem we need to solve?
- Who experiences this problem, and why does it matter?
- What are the constraints (time, budget, materials, skills, safety)?
- What would success look like?
- What do we already know about this problem?

**EngiBuddy Coaching:**
- Push for specificity: "Can you be more specific about the problem?"
- Question assumptions: "Is that assumption based on evidence?"
- Challenge broad scopes: "That's too big for your timeframe. What's the core problem?"
- Ask about stakeholders: "Who would benefit from solving this?"
- Diagnose: Unclear problem definition, missing constraints, wrong problem identified

---

## Phase 2: INVESTIGATE
**Purpose:** Research and gather knowledge about the problem space and potential solutions.

**Characteristics:**
- Conduct literature review and background research
- Gather primary data (interviews, observations, experiments)
- Understand existing solutions
- Identify knowledge gaps and learning needs
- Develop theory or conceptual understanding

**Key Activities:**
- Literature and patent search
- User interviews or surveys
- Experimental exploration
- Comparative analysis of existing solutions
- Building conceptual models

**EngiBuddy Coaching:**
- Question research depth: "What sources did you use? Are they credible?"
- Encourage primary research: "Have you talked to actual users?"
- Ask about gaps: "What don't you understand yet?"
- Challenge premature closing: "Have you considered alternative perspectives?"
- Diagnose: Insufficient research, biased sources, ignoring contradictory evidence, knowledge gaps

---

## Phase 3: PLAN
**Purpose:** Design the approach and create a detailed implementation plan.

**Characteristics:**
- Select the solution approach based on investigation
- Design the solution and its components
- Create Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)
- Allocate resources and establish timeline
- Define testing and validation strategy
- Identify risks and mitigation strategies

**Key Deliverables:**
- Design document or blueprint
- Work Breakdown Structure with dependencies
- Timeline with realistic estimates
- Resource and skill assessment
- Test/validation plan
- Risk register

**EngiBuddy Coaching:**
- Challenge timeline: "How did you estimate this? Is it realistic?"
- Question completeness: "What testing will you do? How will you know if it works?"
- Ask about contingency: "What's your plan if X goes wrong?"
- Encourage detail: "Break this task down further. Is one person really doing all of this?"
- Diagnose: Unrealistic planning, incomplete WBS, missing contingencies, vague specifications

---

## Phase 4: CONSTRUCT
**Purpose:** Build, code, or fabricate the solution according to plan.

**Characteristics:**
- Follow design and plan with flexibility for learning
- Build incrementally with regular integration
- Apply quality practices (version control, testing, documentation)
- Troubleshoot problems systematically
- Adapt plan based on learning

**Key Practices:**
- Incremental development with frequent integration
- Regular testing and validation milestones
- Version control and documentation
- Systematic troubleshooting
- Peer review and feedback

**EngiBuddy Coaching:**
- Ask about integration: "How are you ensuring parts work together?"
- Diagnose blockers: "What specifically is stuck? Have you tried X?"
- Encourage systematic approach: "What debugging steps have you taken?"
- Question documentation: "Are you documenting decisions and lessons?"
- Point out: "You're adding new features. Does that match your plan?"
- Diagnose: Integration failures, knowledge gaps, scope creep, poor troubleshooting

---

## Phase 5: EVALUATE
**Purpose:** Assess the solution against criteria and reflect on the process and learning.

**Characteristics:**
- Test comprehensively against acceptance criteria
- Gather stakeholder/user feedback
- Evaluate success and failures
- Document lessons learned
- Reflect on process, teamwork, and learning
- Prepare documentation and presentation
- Identify improvements and future work

**Key Activities:**
- Comprehensive testing and validation
- Stakeholder demonstration and feedback
- Systematic evaluation against criteria
- Project retrospective and lessons learned
- Portfolio artifact creation
- Presentation preparation

**EngiBuddy Coaching:**
- Ask about evidence: "How did you validate this? What's your evidence?"
- Push reflection: "What did you learn? What surprised you?"
- Challenge shallow evaluation: "You say it works, but against which criteria?"
- Encourage user feedback: "What did your stakeholders/users say?"
- Diagnose: Inadequate testing, shallow reflection, missing user perspective, poor documentation

---

## Barak vs. Hybrid Mapping

| Barak Phase | Hybrid Equivalent | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Identify | Empathize | Problem definition, stakeholder analysis |
| Investigate | Conceive (research part) | Solution exploration, research, learning |
| Plan | Design | Detailed planning and architecture |
| Construct | Implement | Building and execution |
| Evaluate | Test/Revise + Operate | Validation, reflection, learning documentation |

**Key Difference:** Barak emphasizes investigation and systematic problem-solving, with evaluation including significant reflection. Hybrid separates solution exploration (Conceive) from detailed design planning, and breaks validation into testing (phase 5) and broader reflection/deployment (phase 6).

---

## Cross-Phase Principles in Barak

1. **Scientific Thinking:** Each phase involves hypothesis formation and evidence gathering
2. **Iterative Learning:** Expect to revisit phases as understanding deepens
3. **Documentation Throughout:** Record thinking, decisions, and evidence at each phase
4. **User-Centered:** Solution must address the actual problem for actual users
5. **Constraints Drive Solutions:** Work within real limitations, not against them
6. **Systematic Troubleshooting:** When stuck, use scientific method to diagnose
